Frequently asked

Things people ask us.

Can't find your answer? Email kiaora@campshare.co.nz

How is CampShare different from JUCY or Britz?
We don't own a fleet of identical vans. Every van on CampShare belongs to a real person who built it (or had it built) themselves. You'll get a van with character, met at pick-up by someone who can tell you the best free DOC sites on the way. Prices are usually similar or cheaper than big rental companies.
What licence do I need?
A full driver's licence from your home country, held for at least two years. International visitors can drive in NZ for up to 12 months on a current overseas licence; you may also need an International Driving Permit if your licence isn't in English. The minimum age is 21 — but some hosts accept 18+, look on the van listing.
Can I freedom camp?
Only in self-contained vans (with a fixed toilet and grey-water capacity). We've tagged every self-contained van on the platform with a "Freedom camping" badge — use the filter on the browse page. Even then, you can only freedom-camp where signs allow it; the NZMCA and Rankers apps are great for checking.
How does pick-up work?
Your host messages you a few days before. You meet them at their place (or another agreed location), they show you how everything works (water, gas, solar, awning), you sign the hire agreement, and you're off. Most pick-ups take 30–45 minutes.
Can I take the van between islands?
Yes — most hosts allow Interislander ferry crossings. The ferry cost isn't included in the hire price; you book it directly. Check the van listing for any host-specific rules.
How much can I earn hosting?
It depends on your van, where it's based, and how many days you make it available. A typical $175/night self-contained van earning at 100 days a year nets the host around $14,000 after our 20% service fee. Use the calculator on the List your van page.
What if my van gets damaged?
Every booking includes our $2m commercial insurance plus a $2,000 host damage cover. Cosmetic damage, broken fittings, missing gear — we settle these from the guest's bond. Anything bigger is a claim on the commercial policy. You pay nothing.
Do I have to accept every booking?
Never. You see every booking request before it's confirmed and can decline for any reason. Some hosts also set "instant book" for trusted guests only.
Can I block off dates for myself?
Yes. You manage a simple calendar from your dashboard. Block your own holidays, weddings, weekends away — whatever you need. Most hosts use their van for at least 30–60 days a year themselves.
Is the buyback price really guaranteed?
Yes — written into the contract you sign at purchase. The price assumes fair wear and tear and an agreed maximum kilometre allowance (usually 30,000 km / 6 months). Damage beyond fair wear is deducted at a published rate sheet — no surprises.
Who owns the van during the buyback period?
You do. It's registered fully in your name. You can drive across both islands, customise it, take it off-road (if the van's set up for that), even resell it privately if you decide to — though you'd lose the buyback guarantee in that case.
What if I decide I want to keep the van?
Easy — just don't bring it back. You owe us nothing further. The van is yours forever from the day you sign.
What if I crash the van?
You'll have your own comprehensive insurance during the buyback period (we'll help you set this up with our partner brokers). If the van is written off, your insurer pays out the market value to you; the buyback contract is voided. If the van is repaired, the buyback still stands subject to a damage adjustment.
Can I extend the buyback period?
Usually yes, up to a maximum of 12 months from purchase. Email us a month or two before your buyback date — we'll re-quote based on remaining months and kilometres.
What insurance is included in a hire?
Comprehensive cover with a standard $2,000 excess, third-party property cover, windscreen and tyre cover, and 24/7 roadside assistance. You can reduce the excess to $500 for a small daily fee at booking.
What's not covered?
Driving under the influence; off-road driving in vans not set up for it; driving on closed roads or beaches with vehicle restrictions; intentional damage; mechanical wear from misuse (e.g. running on empty). All the usual.
What happens if I break down?
Call our 24/7 line — the number is on a sticker in every van. We dispatch the nearest of our mobile mechanic partners. Most breakdowns are fixed roadside within a few hours. If the van can't be repaired quickly, we'll cover accommodation or arrange a replacement van where possible.
Are the vans actually safe?
Yes. Every van on CampShare is inspected by our team before its first booking, holds a current WOF, and is required to be serviced at least annually. We also do random spot-checks throughout the year. We turn away about 1 in 5 vans that apply — usually for safety reasons we explain to the owner.
