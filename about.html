Our story

We started with one van and the wrong career.

CampShare grew out of a frustrating Christmas in 2021. We'd built our own camper in a Wellington garage, and could never figure out why it spent eleven months a year parked. Meanwhile our mates were renting fleet vans for $300 a night that looked like dental waiting rooms. Something was off.

A platform built by builders, for builders.

We're not Silicon Valley. We're three friends in Wellington, Auckland and Wānaka — one who builds vans, one who used to run a small insurance brokerage, one who's spent more nights in a roof tent than a bed. We made CampShare because nobody had made the thing we wanted to use.

Every van on the platform is hand-built or hand-loved. We've personally inspected most of them. We know our hosts by name, and they know us. When something goes wrong — and it does sometimes — there's a human on the phone within minutes.

We added the buyback programme in 2024 because we kept getting emails from backpackers asking if they could just have the van for six months. Turns out the answer is yes, with a contract.

Our mission

Make travel in Aotearoa slower, smaller and more local.

We want fewer big rental fleets and more people lending each other their stuff. We want travellers to meet hosts and end up with someone's home phone number. We want every van trip to start with a story.
